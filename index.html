Saimou (Simon) Zhang

Ph.D. student in Linguistics · University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign

Broadly speaking, I study how the linguistic and cognitive affordances provided by generative AI shape and are shaped by human-computer collaborative writing processes, across genres and across diverse populations. To do so, I use both experimental and computational approaches.

Currently, I am investigating the conjoined effects of a 3D salience model and cognitive authorship on the reuse of newly introduced named entities within human-computer interactive writing contexts.

My academic training is cross-disciplinary. I started in second language writing, took an interesting detour into sociology and cultural anthropology in education, and later made a quantitative turn through statistics. Nevertheless, questions of coherence and quantitative aesthetics have remained central to my interests, grounded in linguistic theory and newly enriched by LLM-based opportunities.
